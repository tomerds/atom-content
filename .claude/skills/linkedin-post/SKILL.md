# LinkedIn Post

Two sources back this skill. **Read both before writing:**

- `playbooks/linkedin/playbook.md` (13 parts) is Atom's house voice and rules, the
  Stanley playbook. It is the source of truth. On any conflict, the playbook wins.
- `references/linkedin/` is external, web-sourced craft for keeping AI-assisted
  posts from reading as AI slop (the AI tells, the statistical "why," humanization
  technique, hook frameworks). It deepens the playbook; it never overrides it.

The notes below are the quick reference.

## Before writing (always, in this order)

1. **Read the playbook** at `playbooks/linkedin/playbook.md` in full. Match its
   voice, structure, and the do/don't lists over any generic LinkedIn instinct.
2. **Read the external references** in `references/linkedin/`. Apply the AI-slop
   guardrails and hook craft. Where they overlap the playbook, the playbook's
   wording wins.
3. **Read the examples** in `examples/linkedin-posts/` (approved finished posts).
4. **Gather the facts.** What's the source (call, check-in, design-partner
   session, product update, observation)? What's the one insight? Who said what
   (specific quotes, numbers)? What's surprising? Never invent metrics,
   customers, quotes, or features. If a real source is thin, ask.
5. **Pick the matching template** from Part 6 (discovery call / check-in /
   feature / industry observation / lessons-from-customers).

## The non-negotiables (hard rules, Part 2)

- **No em dashes (—).** Ever. Use commas, periods, colons, or parentheses.
- **No bullet points.** Posts flow as prose, never lists.
- **No bold text, no numbered lists.**
- **No engagement bait** ("What do you think?", "Thoughts?", "Drop a comment").
  End with substance.
- **No marketing speak / AI hype** ("game-changer," "transform," "leverage,"
  "AI-powered," "cutting-edge," "seamless"). See the avoid-list in Part 7.3.
- **No artificial transitions** ("But here's the thing," "Let me tell you,"
  "The truth is," "It's not just about X, it's about Y").

## Voice (Part 1.2)

Founder/leader at Atom who genuinely understands research administration from
real conversations. Relaxed, candid, operationally precise, quietly confident,
subtly contrarian, human. Not salesy, not hyped, not preachy. Audience is
research development professionals: direct, skeptical of marketing, appreciative
of specificity. Write what you'd actually say out loud to a colleague.

## Structure (Part 3)

- 150-300 words. Paragraphs of 1-3 sentences. Single-sentence paragraphs for
  emphasis.
- **Hook** (first 1-2 lines, ~210 chars before truncation): lead with a specific
  stat, a situation drop, an unexpected question, or a contrarian statement. Not
  a quote-attribution setup, not a generic observation, not "I've been thinking."
- **Body:** problem→workaround→insight, or story→outcome→pattern, or
  before→after→lesson. Ground every claim in concrete detail (real tools, real
  numbers). Integrate quotes into the flow; one is usually enough, two max; never
  explain a quote afterward.
- **Ending:** forward look, personal reflection, industry warning, or understated
  wisdom. Never a CTA, a summarizing bow, or a hype close.

## Specifics that keep it credible

- Real module/feature names: AI Profile Builder, Proposal Development module, Deep
  Research, AI eligibility matching, AI checklist feature, AI red team review.
- Casual geographic references instead of naming institutions ("an R1 in the
  Midwest"). Never name an institution or individual without approval.
- Never name competitors negatively. Focus on category problems (Part 4.2, 13.2).
- Numbers: spell out one-nine, numerals for 10+; always numerals for %, money,
  time, counts; commas over 999.

## Final pass (Parts 5.3-5.4)

Run the AI-slop filter and self-review checklist before delivering: kill anything
overly polished, generic, or artificially dramatic ("That's not a strategy.
That's survival mode."). Read it out loud, cut anything you wouldn't say.

## Output

Save the draft in a content folder at the repo root named in
`Title_Case_With_Underscores` (e.g. `Survival_Mode_LinkedIn/`), as Markdown.
These folders are gitignored by design. If you write more than one option, label
them clearly in the same file.
